2020 quarterly info: 
[When COVID-19 was in its "prime", the average index and average volume traded for NASDAQ increased, while the
opposite happened for NYSE. This tells us that many were trading in technology-related stocks to take
advantage of the fact that they were low in price and had more potential to grow than traditional stocks both during and
after the pandemic. From this table and the data above, it is clear that the NASDAQ market was both impacted less and
recovered faster than the NYSE market (aka traditional, non-tech stocks). The data also tells us that the stock market
is relatively unreliable in determining the true state of the economy and general welfare, as the unemployment rate
clearly offers a more accurate (and contradicting, with respect to the market) view of what happened during the
different quarters of 2020.]
